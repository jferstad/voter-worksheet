California Primary Neutral Voter Worksheet — Precinct 36013

A local, browser-only app for reviewing source-backed candidate statements, recording your own reactions, and estimating your own candidate fit. It summarizes your ratings without recommending how to vote.

Neutrality note: This is not a recommendation engine. It helps you organize research and your own reactions. Candidate statements in official guides may be paid/submitted by candidates and not independently verified by election officials, so use the linked sources as starting points.

Blinded survey cards

Candidate names stay masked in this section until you reveal them. These cards now exclude bare poll numbers, ballot designations, party labels, and resume-only details. You can rate agreement with each action or policy direction, set how important it is to you, and add notes.

Candidate list used in this worksheet

This is a starter list drawn from the official certified statewide list and San Mateo County candidate roster. Use the 1-5 controls to capture your overall impression for any candidate, including candidates who only have official ballot-designation details so far.

Candidate fit scores

These scores are a worksheet summary of your own inputs: direct 1-5 impressions plus blinded-card agreement ratings weighted by your 0-3 importance choices. They are not predictions, endorsements, or recommendations.

My notes grouped by candidate

This section groups what you entered. Use it as a checklist for who to research further, and use the fit tab for the score summary.

Sources, method, and limitations

This app uses official election materials first, then nonpartisan or mainstream reporting for context. It avoids an automatic “best match” calculation because voting preferences are value judgments and source interpretation can be contested.

How to use this safely

  1. Start with the blinded cards and record your reactions without looking at names.
  2. Reveal identities, then read the linked sources for any candidates you want to investigate.
  3. Check your official sample ballot for races and candidates that are specific to your address.
  4. Treat candidate-supplied statements as claims to verify, not as independent fact checks.
