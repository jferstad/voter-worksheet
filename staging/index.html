Welcome! Let's get you started

This app uses official election materials first, then nonpartisan or mainstream reporting for context. It avoids an automatic “best match” calculation because voting preferences are value judgments and source interpretation can be contested.

How to use this safely

  1. Trusted sources: rate sources so your fit score is based on information you trust.
  2. Blinded survey: react to candidate cards without candidate names.
  3. Fit scores: review the summary created from your own ratings.
  4. Candidate list: reveal identities and research candidates more deeply.
  5. Notes: review what you wanted to verify.
Candidate-selection notes

The candidate list defaults to filtering Governor to candidates named at 1% or higher in the newest poll found during the May 4, 2026 web check. Use “Show full official list” to see every ballot-listed candidate preserved from the official materials.

For local San Mateo County offices, the app includes the candidates visible in the county roster for the offices likely associated with this ballot starter. Because one photo cannot fully verify every address-specific contest, the local section is a research scaffold rather than a final ballot reconstruction.

Choose trusted sources

Rate each source group based on how much you trust it. Fit scores use these choices along with your agreement and importance ratings. Unrated or “don’t know” source groups count the same as “trust a little bit.”

Blinded survey cards

Review each card without candidate names. Rate your agreement, choose how important it is, and add notes if useful.

Candidate list used in this worksheet

This is a starter list drawn from the official certified statewide list and San Mateo County candidate roster. Use the 1-5 controls to capture your overall impression for any candidate, including candidates who only have official ballot-designation details so far.

Candidate fit scores

These scores are a worksheet summary of your own inputs: direct 1-5 impressions plus blinded-card agreement ratings weighted by importance and source trust. They are not predictions, endorsements, or recommendations.
